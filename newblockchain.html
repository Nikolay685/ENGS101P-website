Blockchain Technology

What is Blockchain Technology?

A blockchain is a growing list of records which we call blocks. The blocks are linked and secured using cyrptography. This is only one type of data structure known as a distributed ledger, whereby data is replicated and shared across multiple sites, countries or institutions, requiring a peer to peer network.

Benefits of Blockchain technology

  • Once recorded, the data in any given block cannot be altered without the alteration of all subsequent blocks.
  • A decentralised consensus makes blockchain suitable for the recording of events.
  • Reduced transaction costs by cutting out "middlmen".
  • Complete transparency for the user.

Cryptocurrencies

In its simplest terms, a digital currency are coins that are passed through an electric network. Bitcoin is the first and most famous application of blockchain. Bitcoin uses a peer-to-peer network to broadcast information about transactions, which are then added to blocks that are cryptographically secured, forming an immutable blockchain. The entire blockchain is shared by all participants, therefore it is easy to prevent double-spending. Bitcoin is all about transparency, and thus anyone can check who owns which tokens at which time.

Smart Contracts

These have applications in all sorts of situations, such as financial derivatives, insurance premiums and crowdfunding agreements among others. Smart contracts are embedded with an if-this-then-that (IFTTT) code, which gives them self-execution. The blockchain gets rid of the need for third parties. It also ensures that all ledger participants know the contract details. The contract terms are implemented automatically once the conditions are met.

Stock Exchange

Traditional systems tend to be erro-prone, and often quite slow. Naturally, this costs stress, time, and money. Blockchain technology cuts out the need for any intermediaries, and as such the users find that their experience is cheaper and more transparent. It cuts out the need for brokers and gives every party a say in the validation of the transaction.
