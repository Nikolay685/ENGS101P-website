Numerical pricing of derivatives

1. What is financial derivatives?

Derivatives can be used for a number of purposes, including insuring against price movements (hedging), increasing exposure to price movements for speculation or getting access to otherwise hard-to-trade assets or markets. Generally, derivatives are used to scatter the risks which is contained in the transactions and operating businesses. They are now becoming increasingly vital for the market and the society as a part of the financial market, a sensible way to combine computing and derivatives will be of potentiality.

2. how are the prices of the financial derivatives determined

Typical financial derivatives such as futures and options are used to describe the goods and services which have not been generated and produced yet, so it can possibly be simplified to be guaranteed transactions made in the future. Futures contracts (futures) are financial contracts to buy or sell an underlying commodity at a certain price in the future. Therefore, the futures contract's value is based on the commodity's cash price. For example, consider a corn futures contract that represents 5,000 bushels of corn. If a bushel of corn is trading at $5 per bushel, the value of the contract is $25,000. Futures contracts are standardized to include a certain amount and quality of the underlying commodity, so they can be traded on a centralized exchange. The futures price moves in relation to the spot price for the commodity based on supply and demand for that commodity. Options on stocks and exchange-traded funds are also common derivative contracts. Options give the buyer the right, as opposed to the obligation, to buy or sell 100 shares of a stock at a strike price for a predetermined amount of time. The best-known pricing model for options is the Black-Scholes method. This method considers the underlying stock price, option strike price, time until the option expires, underlying stock volatility and risk-free interest rate to provide a value for the option.

Regardless the obvious prices for the goods and services, a huge number of factors are definitely needed to ensure the precision of the numerical outcome. The dynamic digits of elements like exchange rates, interest rates and the prospects of firms are undoubtedly crucial. However, all these factors will make the predictions extremely sophisticated, it is no longer as simple as just a calculation. Therefore the role in which computer plays is to visualised the pricing processes and to make the prediction as modulus as possible (to make it easier to predict). A platform for pricing financial derivatives called premia is a perfect example of using software as a tool.

4. Links to hedging/hedge fund

Although being slightly different to typical cases, hedging is often brought together with financial derivatives. It just simply indicates a way to offset the potential risks by carrying out the opposite action; for instance, to reduce the impacts brought by the decreasing exchange rates, MNCs (multi-national corporations) usually purchase the counter currency in advance. To accurately estimate the specific action which is needed to be carried out, a chain of calculation and predictions are essential. For the same reason with pricing the typical financial derivatives, simplifications are necessary to make the process workable.
