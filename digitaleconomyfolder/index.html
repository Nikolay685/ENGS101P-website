Three theories of digital economy


Metcalfe's Law: The value of a network is directly proportional to the square of size of the network users.²


Moore's Law: The number of components of circuits doubles around per 2 years when the price of the circuit is unchanged, which means unit cost decreases.³


Davidow's theory : It is necessary that each enterprise in an industry updates products of this enterprise continuously. If an enterprise intends to become dominant in the market of this industry, it has to be the first one developing new generation of the products.⁴
